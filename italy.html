To cook Tagliatelle alla Bolognese

Chop the pancetta. Trim the celery, carrots and onion, and mince into fine pieces. Heat a pan with a drizzle of oil, sauté the pancetta, add the vegetables and gently sweat until soft. Add the meat and sauté for 5 minutes. Add 1/2 cup wine and allow to completely evaporate.

Add the tomato passata and stir for a few minutes. Add 1/2 cup milk and cover with the broth. Cook the meat sauce for 2-3 hours, adding more broth every now and then while adjusting the seasoning. Cook the tagliatelle in boiling salted water for 3-4 minutes or until al dente, drain and serve with generous amounts of sauce.

To make Tiramisu

Beat egg yolks and sugar until pale. Add mascarpone, mix until smooth.

Whip heavy cream to stiff peaks, fold into mascarpone mixture. Dip ladyfingers in coffee (add liqueur if using), arrange a layer in dish.

Spread half of mascarpone mixture over ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture. Chill for at least 4 hours. Dust with cocoa powder before serving.

To Make Caprese Salad

Arrange the Salad: On a serving platter or individual plates, alternate slices of tomatoes and mozzarella cheese, slightly overlapping them. Add Basil Leaves: Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices. You can use whole leaves or tear them into smaller pieces.

Season: Drizzle extra virgin olive oil over the salad, ensuring each slice is lightly coated. Optionally, drizzle some balsamic vinegar over the salad for added flavor. Season with Salt and Pepper: Sprinkle salt and freshly ground black pepper over the salad to taste. Remember that the cheese can be slightly salty, so adjust the seasoning accordingly. Serve: Serve the Caprese Salad immediately, allowing the flavors to meld together at room temperature.

To make Chicken Parmesan

Season chicken with salt and pepper. Coat with flour, then dip in beaten eggs, and coat with breadcrumbs mixed with grated Parmesan cheese.

Fry chicken in olive oil until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer chicken to a baking dish, top with marinara sauce, shredded mozzarella, and grated Parmesan.

Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes until cheese is melted and bubbly. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.
