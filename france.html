To Cook Crêpes

In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour and eggs. Gradually whisk in milk and water until smooth. Stir in melted butter and a pinch of salt. Heat a lightly greased skillet over medium heat. Pour a small amount of batter into the skillet and swirl to spread thinly. Cook until the edges start to lift and the bottom is golden brown, then flip and cook the other side. Repeat with the remaining batter, stacking the cooked crêpes on a plate. Serve warm with your favorite toppings such as Nutella, jam, fresh fruit, or whipped cream. Enjoy your delicious homemade crêpes

To cook Ratatouille

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and cook until softened. Add diced eggplant, zucchinis, bell peppers, and tomatoes to the skillet. Cook until vegetables are tender, stirring occasionally. Stir in tomato paste, dried thyme, and dried basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cook for another 5-10 minutes to allow flavors to meld together. Serve hot or at room temperature, garnished with fresh basil leaves. Ratatouille can be enjoyed on its own or served as a side dish with crusty bread or pasta.

To cook Quiche Lorraine

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a 9-inch pie dish with the pie crust and trim the edges. In a skillet, cook diced bacon until crisp. Remove from skillet and drain on paper towels. In the same skillet, cook chopped onion until translucent. Spread cooked bacon and onion evenly over the bottom of the pie crust. Sprinkle shredded Gruyère cheese on top. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and r, and a pinch of nutmeg. Pour the egg mixture over the bacon, onion, and cheese. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden brown on top.

To cook boeuf bourguignon

Toss together beef pieces, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl; set aside. Cook bacon in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat until fat is rendered and bacon is beginning to brown, 7 to 8 minutes. Add seasoned beef to bacon and cook, stirring often, until well browned, 7 to 8 minutes. Add onion and cook, stirring often, until beginning to soften, 2 to 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute. Add mushrooms and tomato paste to mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, for 4 minutes. Stir in broth, wine, and carrots and bring mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to low. Cover and cook for 40 to 45 minutes or until beef is fork-tender. Uncover and remove 1/2 cup broth to a 1-cup glass measuring cup. Whisk in flour until completely combined and smooth.
