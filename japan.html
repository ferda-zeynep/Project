To cook Teriyaki chicken

Butterfly chicken thigh fillets to make the fillet an even thickness. Using the tip of a knife, poke the skin randomly. Heat a frying pan and cook the chicken, skin side down. Turn the chicken over and cook further. Add the Teriyaki Sauce to the pan and cook until the sauce thickens. Transfer the chicken to a cutting board and cover with a sheet of foil. Cut the chicken into bite-sized strips.

To cook Ramen

In a large pot, combine chicken broth and pork broth and bring to a simmer over medium heat. Add sesame oil, soy sauce, miso paste, grated ginger, and minced garlic. Stir well to combine. Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions and drain. Divide cooked noodles into serving bowls and ladle the hot broth over them. Top with sliced pork belly, soft-boiled egg, sliced green onions, bamboo shoots, nori, and corn kernels. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Tonkotsu Ramen!

To Make Sushi

Cook sushi rice according to package instructions and let it cool slightly. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top. Arrange avocado slices, cucumber strips, and crab sticks in the center of the rice. Carefully roll the sushi using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure to shape it into a cylinder. Wet the top border of the nori with a little water to seal the roll. Slice the roll into bite-sized pieces using a sharp knife. Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over the California rolls. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger. Enjoy your homemade California Rolls!

To cook Okonomiyaki(Japanese pancake)

In a large bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour and dashi until smooth. Add shredded cabbage, sliced green onions, chopped shrimp, chopped bacon, and eggs to the batter. Mix until well combined. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly oil the surface. Pour the batter onto the skillet to form a pancake about 1/2 inch thick. Cook for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer the cooked okonomiyaki to a plate and drizzle with okonomiyaki sauce and mayonnaise. Sprinkle bonito flakes and dried seaweed flakes on top. Serve hot and enjoy your Osaka-style Okonomiyaki!
