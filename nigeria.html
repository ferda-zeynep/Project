To Cook Jollof Rice

Cook the onions in the olive oil in a large pot until softened. Then add the garlic and ginger and cook for 30 seconds to release their fragrance. Stir frequently so they don’t burn! Add the rice and stir it in the pot to toast it. Add the stock, crushed tomatoes, seasoned salt, smoked paprika, curry powder, thyme, paprika, and bay leaf to the pot. Stir everything together and once it boils, cover it with a lid and cook until the liquid evaporates and the rice is tender. Turn off the heat and allow the rice to rest with the lid on. Remove the bay leaf and stir everything together. Serve it up garnished with parsley and bay leaves.

To Cook Suya

Thread thinly sliced beef or chicken onto skewers. Rub peanut oil over the meat, then coat generously with suya spice mix. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until cooked through and slightly charred. Serve hot, garnished with sliced onions and tomatoes. Enjoy your flavorful Suya!

To Cook Puff Puff

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Gradually add the warm water to the dry ingredients, stirring continuously until a smooth, thick batter forms. The consistency should be similar to that of pancake batter. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and let the batter rest in a warm place for about 1 hour, allowing it to rise and double in size. In a deep pot or fryer, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C) over medium-high heat. Using a spoon or your hands, scoop up a small portion of the batter and carefully drop it into the hot oil. Repeat with more batter, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Fry the puff puff until golden brown on all sides, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. This usually takes about 3-5 minutes. Remove the fried puff puff from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

To cook Egusi soup

Soak 1 cup of egusi seeds in water for about 5 minutes. Drain and blend until smooth. Set aside. In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of palm oil over medium heat. Sauté 1 chopped onion until translucent. Add 2 chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until softened. Stir in the blended egusi and 2 tablespoons of ground crayfish. Cook, stirring constantly, for 5-7 minutes. Add 500g of assorted meat to the pot and cook until browned. Pour in 2 cups of vegetable or chicken broth. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Stir in 2 cups of chopped spinach or bitter leaf and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
